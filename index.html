03 · Questions to Ask

At the Showroom

Eight questions — and why each one matters

Pricing

Can we get a full itemized written quote today, or do we need to follow up with a sales rep?

Why ask?

Reviews say prices are never posted. You need this in writing before you can commit to anything.

Chairs

What's the exact per-unit price difference between Bistro Walnut and Crossback Weathered Oak at 85 units?

Why ask?

We've estimated $200–$400 savings with Bistro. Confirm the actual gap at volume before deciding.

Delivery

What's the delivery fee for our venue? Is there a minimum order for free or discounted delivery?

Why ask?

Budgeted at $200–$500 — a wide range. Nailing this down tightens the total significantly.

Damage Waiver

Is the ~10% damage waiver mandatory? What exactly does it cover — and what doesn't it cover?

Why ask?

Non-refundable and auto-added. Covers accidents, not lost items or intentional damage. Budget ~$238–$333.

Rental Period

Our event is Saturday June 27. Is the rental window Fri–Mon? What does Sat + Sun counting as 1 day mean?

Why ask?

Standard is 3 days, but Sat+Sun = 1 day. Clarifies pickup, return, and whether extra days cost more.

Serveware

What bowl and platter shapes do you have for family-style pasta mains? We need to confirm vessel depth for carbonara.

Why ask?

Still an open question with our caterer. Don't finalize this order until depth is confirmed.

Linens

Do you stock the 156×108 tablecloth for 8ft tables? What colors and fabric options are available?

Why ask?

Color selection is worth deciding in person — photos don't capture texture or scale.

Delivery Reliability

If something is missing or damaged on delivery day, what's the remedy process? Who do we call?

Why ask?

Reviews flag delivery as hit-or-miss. Know the contingency plan before you need it.

04 · Watch-Outs

Don't Get Blindsided

Six things to know before you walk in

No Published Prices — Ever

BPR posts nothing online. Staff will give relative guidance but won't commit to numbers without a sales rep. Multiple reviews flag this as a frustration. Push for a full itemized written quote before you leave — verbal estimates evaporate.

The Damage Waiver Is Non-Negotiable

~10% of your order total, added automatically, non-refundable. Covers accidental breakage — broken plates, cracked glasses. Does not cover lost items or intentional damage. Budget ~$238–$333 on top of the subtotal.

Delivery Reliability Is Mixed

Reviews flag delivery as the weakest part of the BPR experience. Items have arrived late or incomplete. Confirm: delivery window, venue address on file, who to call day-of, and what the remedy process looks like.

Serveware Has an Unresolved Question

The vessel for Kimchi & Uni Carbonara — bowl vs. platter — hasn't been confirmed with STE Events. The issue is depth: carbonara needs a vessel that holds liquid. Don't finalize the serveware order until the caterer signs off.

Saturday + Sunday Count as One Rental Day

BPR's standard period is 3 days, but weekends count as one. For June 27, the likely window is Fri delivery → Mon return. Confirm this explicitly — exact dates affect logistics with your venue.

Table Count Is Still TBD

Estimating 9–10 tables based on 75 guests at 8-person 8ft tables. Final count depends on venue floor plan. Don't place the furniture order until that's confirmed — tables and chairs are the most expensive line item.

05 · The Showroom Experience

What to Expect

How the visit works and how to use it wisely

You'll Work With a Sales Rep

The floor is consultative — someone walks you through the catalog. They'll give relative pricing but won't commit to numbers without paperwork. Ask to speak with a rep who can issue a formal written quote on the spot.

You Can See & Touch the Pieces

This is the best reason to go in person. Compare the Bistro Walnut and Crossback Oak chairs side by side. Check serveware dimensions — bring the caterer's menu as a reference for vessel sizing.

Nothing Gets Ordered Without Paperwork

BPR won't ship anything without a signed contract and deposit. The visit is for scoping and quoting — you're not locked in until you sign. Use this trip to gather numbers, not to commit.

Get Everything in Writing

Before you leave: confirm you have a line-item quote with per-unit pricing, delivery fee, damage waiver amount, and rental period spelled out. Verbal quotes don't survive the gap between showroom and invoice.

Linen Colors Are a Real Decision

The 156×108 tablecloth comes in multiple colors and fabrics. Photos online don't capture texture or how it reads at table length. Bring your color direction — worth deciding in the room.

Get a Named Contact Before You Leave

Delivery inconsistency is the #1 complaint in reviews. Leave with a specific rep's name and direct number. "Call the main line" is not good enough for a June 27 wedding.
